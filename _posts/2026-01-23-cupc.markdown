## TDDFT
\begin{equation}
\delta \rho(r,t)=\chi(r,r^\prime,t-t^\prime)\delta v_{ext}(r^\prime,t^\prime)
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
\delta \rho (r,t)= \chi_{irr}(r,r^\prime,t-t^\prime)\delta v_{s}(r^\prime,t^\prime)
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
\delta v_s(t)=\delta v_{ext}(t)+\delta v_H(t)+\delta v_{xc}(t)
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
V_{ind}=\delta v_H(t)+\delta v_{xc}(t)
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
V_{ind}(r)=f_{Hxc}(r-r^\prime)\delta \rho(r^\prime)
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\epsilon \chi=\chi_{irr}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\epsilon=\delta-\chi_{irr}f_{Hxc}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\chi=\epsilon^{-1}\chi_{irr}
\end{equation}
To find the electronic excitattions of the system, we 
have to determin the poles of the $$\chi$$
We can find two different ways in which poles can arise.
First, the poles of $$\chi_{irr}$$. Second $$\epsilon$$ has 
an zero mode.
The first kind is single-particle excitations,wheras the second type is plasmons.

To find the pole of $$\chi$$ is equal to find zero mode of $$\chi^{-1}=\chi_{irr}^{-1}\epsilon=\chi_{irr}^{-1}\delta -f_{Hxc}$$


Now we expand those variables in basis set
\begin{equation}
\delta \rho= P_{ia}\phi_i\phi_a^* + P_{ai}\phi_a\phi_i
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\chi_{irr}(\omega)=\frac{\delta n_{st}}{\epsilon_s-\epsilon_t-\omega}
\end{equation}

## NTO
Single Particle transition density
\begin{equation}
T_{ia}=\langle \Psi_{ex}\vert c^\dagger_a c_i \vert \Psi_0\rangle
\end{equation}
index  i occupied orbitals and a virtual orbitals
T is $N_o\times N_v$ matrix 
\begin{equation}
T=U\lambda V
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\phi_1,\phi_2,\dots \phi_{N_o}=(\psi_1,\cdots,\psi_{N_o})U
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\phi_1,\phi_2,\dots \phi_{N_v}=(\psi_1,\cdots,\psi_{N_a})V
\end{equation}
So there is particle orbital and hole orbitals.and eigenvalue $$\lambda$$
